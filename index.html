2025 · Cedaris Technologies LLC

Ember

Early fire detection using a custom YOLOv8 model trained to distinguish live flames from safe sources like candles, stovetops, and sunlight. Paired with a UV sensor and wake-interrupt logic so the device stays dormant until UV activity crosses a threshold, then wakes to classify and confirm before alerting. Built on Raspberry Pi, with prototyping toward a dedicated embedded board with battery support.

2026 · Machine Learning

SkullNet

A 3-class locomotor ecology classifier trained on depth map renders of mammal skulls. EfficientNetB0 via transfer learning, 26 species from MorphoSource, 4 orthographic angles per skull. 72% accuracy on aquatic / arboreal / terrestrial — well above the 33% random baseline. Applied speculatively to extinct taxa including Triceratops horridus.

2026 · Cedaris Technologies LLC

Rail Safety — V.01 Concepts

Concept work for AI-assisted anomaly detection on freight rail — dragging equipment, overheated bearings, structural faults detectable before failure. Two architectures explored: a stationary trackside sensor array and an on-car unit. The trackside approach won out as a starting point because it requires no hardware on rolling stock, making it far easier to prototype and gather training data. The on-car unit stalled on three unsolved problems: no clear power source, no good mounting point on a standard car, and no reliable way to get data off the unit while in transit. Both architectures are documented with open problems preserved.

Purdue University, Spring 2025

DataFest 2025

Won Best Business Insights out of 15 teams at the ASA DataFest at Purdue, a 48-hour competition working with a real-world commercial real estate dataset. As a two-person team, we used regression modeling and k-means clustering in Python to segment markets by occupancy trends, pricing anomalies, and regional demand signals. That let us flag undervalued markets that looked unattractive on headline metrics but showed strong underlying demand. We presented the findings as investment recommendations to a panel of judges.

Personal

Born and raised in Lake Tahoe, I spend my free time hiking and exploring. I love rock hounding, fossil hunting, foraging and fishing. In the Sierra Nevada, I enjoy hiking to old mines and learning about their history. In the Midwest, I enjoy looking for fossils and rocks and caring for my many plants. I find myself noticing the same patterns in both — learning to read a place takes the same kind of attention whether you are debugging a sensor or identifying a formation.

California, Summer 2025

Heritage Trout Challenge

California's Heritage Trout Challenge, run by the Department of Fish and Wildlife, asks anglers to catch six of the state's native trout species, each in its historic watershed from the high Sierra to the remote north. I pursued and finished it in the summer of 2025, working through the Golden Trout Wilderness, Lassen, the Meiss country, and the McCloud and Warner drainages.
