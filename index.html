2025 · Cedaris Technologies LLC

Ember

Early fire detection using a custom YOLOv8 model trained to distinguish live flames from safe light sources like candles, stovetops, and sunlight. Paired with a UV sensor and wake-interrupt logic so the device stays dormant until UV activity crosses a threshold, then wakes to classify and confirm before alerting. Built on Raspberry Pi, with prototyping toward a dedicated embedded board with battery support. Provisional patent filed October 2025.

2026 · Cedaris Technologies LLC

Rail Safety — V.01 Concepts

Early-stage concept work for AI-assisted anomaly detection on freight rail. Two architectures explored: a stationary trackside sensor array that passively inspects every passing car, and an on-car unit that monitors continuously. Both are concept sketches with open problems and reasoning preserved. The trackside unit is the more tractable starting point. The on-car unit ran into a cluster of unsolved problems around power and data collection and was set aside.

California, Summer 2025

Heritage Trout Challenge

California's Heritage Trout Challenge, run by the Department of Fish and Wildlife, asks anglers to catch six of the state's native trout species, each in its historic watershed from the high Sierra to the remote north. It's a lifetime pursuit for most people. I finished it in the summer of 2025, working through the Golden Trout Wilderness, Lassen, the Meiss country, and the McCloud and Warner drainages. Six species, a lot of miles, and country I wouldn't have found any other way.

California Golden Trout — Kern drainage
Lahontan Cutthroat — Meiss country
Goose Lake Redband — Lassen
Warner Valley — Dismal Creek
McCloud River Redband
Rainbow Trout — Silver Lake
