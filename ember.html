Ember

A smarter, faster way to detect fire before it spreads.

"Ember" is a working name used descriptively; no trademark claim is asserted.

Ember was a genuine attempt to build something: a hardware startup, a provisional patent, a company. It didn't continue, not for lack of care, but because the tradeoffs of pursuing it seriously at this point in my life didn't add up. What's left is a working prototype, a set of ideas that held up under scrutiny, and a clearer sense of what it actually takes to go from concept to product.

Problem

Most smoke alarms only respond once smoke fills the air, often too late. Ember detects ignition right away, giving people more time to act and stay safe.

Solution

Ember uses paired sensors and onboard processing to recognize flames instantly, even in bright or changing light. It can tell the difference between a real fire and safe light sources like candles or stovetops.

Technology

Wake-interrupt logic monitors UV activity continuously, activating only when distinct flame signatures appear. When a potential ignition is detected, it wakes to analyze and confirm before triggering an alert, saving energy while staying responsive.

Value Proposition

Earlier detection saves lives and property. Ember brings a modern approach to a problem that hasn't meaningfully evolved in decades.

Market

Home fires affect hundreds of thousands of families every year. The core product is a drop-in replacement for existing smoke alarms with meaningfully better detection.
